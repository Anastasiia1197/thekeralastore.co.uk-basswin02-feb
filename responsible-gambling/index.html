Identifying warning signs before problems arise

Recognizing early signs of risky behaviour is crucial for maintaining control. Be mindful if you find yourself spending more time or money than intended, thinking constantly about gaming, or neglecting work, family, or social responsibilities. Borrowing money to gamble, feeling restless or irritable when not playing, and trying to win back losses are strong warning signals. If you notice these patterns in yourself or someone close to you, it's time to pause and seek support.

Debunking common gambling myths and misconceptions

Many misconceptions about gambling can lead to risky behaviour. A common myth is that a win is "due" after a series of losses—this is known as the gambler's fallacy. Each game round is independent and random. Another myth is that certain strategies can guarantee wins in games of chance. While skill can improve odds in some games, chance always plays a significant role. Understanding that the house always has a statistical edge helps set realistic expectations for players in the UK.
